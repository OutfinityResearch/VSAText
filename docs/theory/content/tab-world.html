World Tab

The World tab defines the rules that govern how your story's universe operates. These world rules ensure consistency and set reader expectations.

When to Use This Tab

Use the World tab when building speculative fiction (fantasy, sci-fi) or any story with rules that differ from our reality. Define what's possible before writing scenes.

What This Tab Does

The World tab lets you establish:

  • Physical laws and their exceptions
  • Magic systems and their limitations
  • Technology levels and capabilities
  • Social structures and norms
  • Biological rules for creatures
  • Temporal rules and prophecies

World Rule Categories

Physics

How the physical world operates differently from reality.

Example Rule Story Impact
"Gravity is 50% of Earth's" Characters can jump higher, different architecture
"Light cannot escape the void" Regions of absolute darkness
"Sound carries for miles" Stealth is nearly impossible

Magic System

Rules governing supernatural abilities.

Example Rule Story Impact
"Magic requires sacrifice" Every spell has a cost, moral weight
"Spells must rhyme" Verbal component, can be interrupted
"Only women can channel" Gender dynamics, power structures
"Magic fades at dawn" Time pressure, night-focused action

Technology

Rules for technological capabilities and limits.

Example Rule Story Impact
"FTL travel causes temporal displacement" Time dilation, returning to changed world
"AI is banned" Manual labor, human-centered society
"Communication is instantaneous" No information asymmetry

Society

Rules for social structures and norms.

Example Rule Story Impact
"Caste determines profession" Characters fight against destiny
"Dragons and humans coexist" Interspecies dynamics, communication
"Names have power" True names kept secret, naming rituals

Biology

Rules for creatures and life forms.

Example Rule Story Impact
"Vampires cannot enter homes uninvited" Threshold protection, social manipulation
"Elves live 1000 years" Long-term planning, different values
"Werewolves transform monthly" Predictable danger periods

Time & Fate

Rules for time, destiny, and prophecy.

Example Rule Story Impact
"Time flows differently in the fae realm" Characters return to changed world
"Prophecies always come true" Fate is inescapable, interpretation matters
"The dead cannot be resurrected" Death has finality, high stakes

Step-by-Step: Creating a World Rule

  1. Click "Add Rule"
  2. Select the category (physics, magic, etc.)
  3. Write the rule clearly and concisely
  4. Optionally add exceptions or conditions
  5. Note implications for the story
  6. Save the rule

Metrics Impact

World rules affect World Consistency metrics:

  • Scenes should not violate established rules
  • Character actions must respect world limitations
  • Objects should behave according to world physics

Sanderson's Laws of Magic

Consider these principles when designing magic systems:

  1. First Law: Magic should have clear limitations to create interesting problems
  2. Second Law: Flaws and limitations are more interesting than powers
  3. Third Law: Expand what you have before adding something new

Best Practices

  1. Be specific: Vague rules lead to inconsistencies
  2. Include costs: Every power should have limitations
  3. Think implications: How does each rule affect society?
  4. Keep it minimal: 5-10 key rules is usually enough
  5. Reference in scenes: Rules should be visible in action
  6. Don't break your rules: Or explain why you can
