Themes Tab

The Themes tab manages the underlying messages and ideas your story explores. Themes give stories meaning beyond the plot.

When to Use This Tab

Use the Themes tab to intentionally design what your story is about at a deeper level. Select themes early and let them guide character arcs and plot choices.

What This Tab Does

The Themes tab helps you:

  • Select primary and secondary themes
  • View theme descriptions and examples
  • See suggested story blocks for each theme
  • Connect themes to character arcs
  • Ensure thematic coherence across the story

Theme vs Topic

Understanding the difference is crucial:

Topic (Subject) Theme (Message)
Love "True love requires sacrifice"
Power "Absolute power corrupts absolutely"
War "War destroys the innocent first"

Step-by-Step: Setting Themes

  1. Click "Select Themes"
  2. Choose one primary theme (main focus)
  3. Optionally select 1-2 secondary themes
  4. Review suggested blocks and moods
  5. Consider how themes connect to character arcs
  6. Save theme selection

Metrics Impact

Themes affect several metrics:

  • Thematic Coherence: Does the story consistently explore its themes?
  • Theme Expression: Are themes visible in character actions?
  • Resolution Alignment: Does the ending address thematic questions?

Best Practices

  1. Limit to 2-3 themes: Too many dilutes impact
  2. Show, don't tell: Themes emerge from action, not exposition
  3. Connect to protagonist: Hero's journey should embody theme
  4. Use contrast: Different characters can represent different views
  5. Address in climax: Resolution should answer thematic question
