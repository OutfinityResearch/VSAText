Blueprint Tab

The Blueprint tab provides high-level story planning tools that connect abstract narrative structure to concrete content. This is where you design the overall shape of your story.

When to Use This Tab

Use the Blueprint tab early in your planning process to establish the narrative arc, map story beats, design tension curves, and plan subplots.

What This Tab Does

The Blueprint tab is your story's architectural plan. It defines:

  • Which narrative arc template guides your story
  • How story beats map to specific chapters and scenes
  • The tension curve showing emotional intensity over time
  • Subplot threads and their intersection points

Blueprint Components

1. Arc Selection

Choose the narrative arc template that best fits your story:

Arc Beats Best For Origin
Hero's Journey 12 Epic adventures, transformations Joseph Campbell
Three Act Structure 9 General fiction, screenplays Aristotle/Hollywood
Save the Cat 15 Commercial fiction, detailed plotting Blake Snyder
Story Circle 8 Character-focused stories Dan Harmon
Kishotenketsu 4 Stories without central conflict Japanese tradition
Five Act 5 Classical drama, tragedies Freytag
Seven-Point 7 Novels with clear turning points Dan Wells

2. Beat Mapping

Assign arc beats to specific locations in your story:

Property Description Example
Beat The narrative beat name "Call to Adventure"
Chapter Target chapter Chapter 2
Scene Specific scene (optional) Scene 3
Tension Tension level (1-5) 3
Notes Author notes "Herald delivers message"

3. Tension Curve

The tension curve is a visual editor for emotional intensity across your story. Each point defines:

  • Position: Where in the story (0.0 = start, 1.0 = end)
  • Tension: Intensity level (1-5)

A well-designed tension curve typically:

  • Starts at low tension (ordinary world)
  • Rises through the first half
  • Has a midpoint shift
  • Peaks near 75-85% (climax)
  • Falls to resolution

4. Subplots

Secondary story threads that enrich the main narrative:

Property Description Options
Name Subplot identifier Any descriptive name
Type Subplot pattern romance, rivalry, mystery, growth, revenge, mentorship
Characters Who is involved Select from defined characters
Start Beat When it begins Any story beat
Resolve Beat When it concludes Any story beat
Touchpoints Main plot intersections List of scenes/chapters

Metrics Impact

Blueprint decisions affect several key metrics:

  • Emotional Arc Profile (EAP): How well scene moods match the tension curve
  • Beat Coverage: Are all selected arc beats represented in scenes?
  • Subplot Integration: Do subplots have sufficient touchpoints?
  • Pacing Score: Is tension distributed appropriately?

Best Practices

  1. Choose arc early: The arc selection influences everything else
  2. Map major beats first: Start with Inciting Incident, Midpoint, Climax
  3. Don't skip beats: Each beat serves a purpose in the structure
  4. Match subplot to main plot: Subplots should echo or contrast the main theme
  5. Use tension curve: Visual pacing prevents monotony
