Dialogues Tab

The Dialogues tab structures conversations between characters. In SCRIPTA, dialogues are specifications that define purpose and flow, not full scripts.

When to Use This Tab

Use the Dialogues tab when you need to plan important conversations - revelations, confrontations, confessions, or any scene where character interaction is central.

What This Tab Does

The Dialogues tab helps you structure conversations by defining:

  • Purpose of the conversation
  • Participants and their roles
  • Emotional tone and tension level
  • Key exchanges and their intent
  • Connection to narrative beats

Dialogue Properties

Property Description Options
Name Dialogue identifier Descriptive name
Purpose Why this conversation happens reveal, conflict, bonding, exposition, negotiation, confession, argument, farewell
Participants Characters involved List of characters with roles
Tone Emotional register confrontational, intimate, formal, casual, tense, humorous
Tension Conflict level (1-5) 1 = calm, 5 = explosive
Beat Link Associated narrative beat Any beat from selected arc

Dialogue Purposes

Purpose Function Typical Tension Example
reveal Hidden truth emerges 3-4 "I am your father"
conflict Direct opposition 4-5 Hero confronts villain
bonding Relationship building 1-2 Friends sharing stories
exposition World/plot information 1-2 Mentor explains quest
negotiation Seeking agreement 2-4 Alliance forming
confession Admitting truth 3-4 Revealing love or guilt
argument Heated disagreement 4-5 Friends falling out
farewell Parting words 2-3 Before the final battle

Dialogue Exchanges

Each dialogue contains exchanges - outline entries for what characters convey:

Property Description Example
Speaker Who is speaking Anna
Intent What they want to convey "demands the truth"
Emotion How they feel anger, desperation
Sketch Rough version of the line "Tell me what really happened!"

Step-by-Step: Creating a Dialogue

  1. Click "Add Dialogue"
  2. Give it a descriptive name
  3. Select the purpose
  4. Add participating characters
  5. Set the tone and tension level
  6. Link to a narrative beat (optional)
  7. Add exchanges:
    • Select speaker
    • Describe their intent
    • Note their emotion
    • Optionally sketch the line
  8. Save the dialogue

Metrics Impact

Dialogues affect the Dialogue Quality Score (dialogueQuality):

  • Well-defined dialogues have clear purpose
  • Multiple participants create dynamic exchanges
  • Substantive exchanges with clear intent
  • Empty or placeholder dialogues reduce the score

Dialogue-Scene Connection

Dialogues can be assigned to scenes:

Sc7 includes dialogue D3

This ensures the dialogue's emotional tone aligns with the scene mood.

Best Practices

  1. One purpose per dialogue: Don't mix revelation with bonding
  2. Keep exchanges focused: 3-7 exchanges per dialogue
  3. Vary tension: Not every dialogue needs to be explosive
  4. Use intent, not full lines: Leave room for generation
  5. Link to beats: Major dialogues should align with arc
  6. Consider character voice: Traits should influence speech
